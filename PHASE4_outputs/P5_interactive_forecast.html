About this dashboard. This interactive viewer presents MDR rate forecasts (2025–2030) for 11 nosocomial neuroinvasive bacterial pathogens across 149 organism-country time series built from Pfizer ATLAS data (2004–2024). MDR is defined per Magiorakos et al. (CMI 2012) as non-susceptible (R or I) to ≥3 antibiotic classes. Forecasting uses ARIMA (order selected by AIC minimisation with ADF stationarity test; Box & Jenkins 1976; Hyndman & Athanasopoulos 2021) and Prophet (piecewise linear trend, no seasonality; Taylor & Letham 2018). Only series with ≥8 valid yearly data points (≥10 isolates per cell, WHO GLASS 2023) are included. The 50% MDR threshold is the WHO GLASS 2023 / ECDC EARS-Net 2023 high-burden alert level.

Views: Forecast Lines — historical (solid) + ARIMA (dashed) + Prophet (dotted) per country with optional 95% CI shading. Ensemble + CI Bands — ARIMA+Prophet ensemble mean (diamond markers) with outer uncertainty envelope. Alert Summary — organism overview grid + per-organism threshold alert table distinguishing already in crisis (>50% MDR in 2024) from emerging crisis (projected to cross 50% by 2030).

Use organism buttons (full names, with country count in brackets) to switch pathogens. Country Filter isolates a single country trajectory. Show/Hide toggles control which model lines are displayed.

⚠ Excluded organisms: Citrobacter koseri (474 isolates spread across 48 countries — no country reached ≥8 valid yearly data points) and Salmonella spp (only 8 total isolates in the filtered dataset) had insufficient temporal data for ARIMA/Prophet modelling. ⚠ Low coverage: Haemophilus influenzae has only 1 qualifying series — results are not generalisable across countries.

Data source: Pfizer ATLAS Surveillance Programme 2004–2024. MDR definition: Magiorakos et al. CMI 2012.
